Visualizing Need for Housing In Pennslyvania: an interactive data index

There are static snapshots of housing pressures, and tables summarizing housing supply needs, but no tool to display how needs are changing over time across the state. It is important for Pennsylvania policymakers to know where housing assistance is needed and whether affordable housing exists to meet those needs. A tool that illustrates this story will help policymakers make better funding allocation and policy decisions in this resource-constrained environment.

Using American Community Survey Public Use Microdata Sample (PUMS), we will make a choropleth map of Pennsylvania that changes over time to reflect changing need. Symbolized on the map will be data that tells the story of where people with the greatest housing burdens (or least affordability) are living in the state. These data will be visible for renters or homeowners and a simple toggle will allow the viewer to shift between the two. The view can then (de)select data layers to see layers separately, or join them together into an index so that they can be viewed simulataneously. A slider timeline along the bottom of the map will allow the viewer to see changes over time. Clicking on an individual county (or PUMA) shape will uncover more information about how the % change in renter/homeowner population has changed compared to the % change in rental/owner-occupied units.
