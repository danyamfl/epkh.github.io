Is the Middle Class Left Out in Pennsylvania?

A Look at Middle Class Housing Burden in the Keystone State


Starting with the Great Recession and increasing with the 2016 Presidential Election, people across the United States started to realize that large parts of the US were being left out of the political and social narratives.
With a large middle class, a history as a successful rustbelt state, and home to an increasingly polarized rural and urban population, Pennsylvania is a state that epitomizes these 21st century tensions.

Housing burden is generally something examined for the traditionally most vulnerable households to determine what housing support they should receive from the government. But how is this middle class faring without the support of the government safety net? Are their costs going up faster than their incomes? Are middle class households in rural areas experiencing different housing burdens than those in urban areas?
