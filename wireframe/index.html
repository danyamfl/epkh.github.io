How Has Housing Burden Changed Over Time for Low-Income Households in Pennsylvania?

A look at the Keystone State


Pennsylvania is experiencing tension between lower-density rural areas becoming more conservative and more dense metropolitan areas becoming more liberal. An aging, rural population is colliding with the influx of a new industry – hydraulic fracturing - and the workers who come with it. Examining the distribution of poverty and housing burden in Pennsylvania over time will help guide Harrisburg policymakers as they make decisions about how to best support the most vulnerable Pennsylvanians during this time of change.

Households with incomes below the poverty line have less money to spend on everything they need, and that includes their own homes. Housing cost burden is a measure of what percent of a household’s total income is spent on housing, whether that is rent or a mortgage, taxes, insurance, and utilities.
